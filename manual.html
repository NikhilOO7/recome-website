Getting Started

Installation

RecoMe works on Chrome, Edge, Brave, and any Chromium-based browser.

1

Visit the Chrome Web Store

Search for "RecoMe" or use the direct install link from our website.

2

Click "Add to Chrome"

Grant the requested permissions. RecoMe needs storage (to save your preferences locally), tabs (to detect which site you're on), and activeTab (to read page content for scoring).

3

Pin the Extension

Click the puzzle icon in Chrome's toolbar, then pin RecoMe for quick access. The icon shows the RecoMe logo.

i
System Requirements

Chrome 109+, Edge 109+, Brave, or any Chromium-based browser. No account or server connection required.

Blocking & Boosting

The core of RecoMe: tell it what you want to see less (or more) of.

🚫 Blocking Topics

Blocked topics are permanently hidden across all platforms. Content matching a blocked topic is immediately suppressed from your feed.

How to block:
  • Type a topic in the Dashboard's "Quick Block" field
  • Click the 🚫 button on any content card in your feed
  • Tell the agent: "Block crypto" in the chat
  • Use the keyboard shortcut (Ctrl+Shift+B / Cmd+Shift+B)

⭐ Boosting Topics

Boosted topics get priority visibility. Content matching a boosted topic is highlighted with a "Boosted" badge and surfaced more prominently.

How to boost:
  • Type a topic in the Dashboard's "Quick Like" field
  • Click the 👍 button on any content card in your feed
  • Tell the agent: "I want to see more travel content"
💡
Tip: You can unblock or un-boost topics anytime from the Settings panel. Just click the X next to any topic in the Blocked or Liked lists.

Intelligence

Smart Scoring

Every piece of content goes through RecoMe's multi-factor scoring pipeline to determine what you see.

Scoring Factors

1
Direct Block Check

Is this topic on your block list? If yes, hide immediately.

→
2
Pattern Matching

Does this match a learned behavior pattern (e.g., "avoids clickbait")?

→
3
Graph Affinity

How related is this topic to things you like or dislike in the knowledge graph?

→
4
Context Fit

Does this match your current context (time of day, platform, activity)?

→
5
Recency

Favor fresh content over stale recommendations.

Score Actions

All factors combine into a final score from -1 to +1, which maps to an action:

Hide Strongly negative
Suppress Mildly negative
Show Neutral
Boost Positive

The Aggressiveness slider in Settings controls where the hide/suppress thresholds sit. Higher aggressiveness means more content is hidden.

Pattern Learning

RecoMe automatically detects recurring patterns in your behavior and creates "mid-term cards" that influence future scoring.

1

You interact with content (block, like, skip, dwell)

↓
2

Events accumulate per topic

↓
3

After 3 similar events, a pattern is detected

↓
4

A mid-term card is created (e.g., "block_pattern: clickbait")

↓
5

Future content is scored against these patterns

💡
Memory Decay: Old patterns naturally fade over time with a 30-day half-life. This means RecoMe adapts as your interests change. Recent interactions always carry more weight than older ones.

Implicit Learning

RecoMe doesn't just learn from your explicit actions. It also picks up on subtle behavioral signals to refine your preferences.

Signal How it's detected Strength
Short Dwell Content visible for 7+ seconds Mild interest
Extended Dwell Content visible for 30+ seconds Strong interest
Click-through Clicked a recommendation link Mild interest
Scroll Past Quickly scrolled past content Low interest
Revisit Returned to content within 24 hours Strong interest
Search Query Searched for a specific topic Moderate interest

Implicit signals have lower confidence (0.4-0.6) than explicit actions (1.0), so they gently influence scoring without overriding your stated preferences.

Troubleshooting

RecoMe isn't hiding anything on a site
  1. Check that the platform is enabled in Settings
  2. Make sure RecoMe isn't toggled off (check for "OFF" badge on the icon)
  3. The site may not have recognizable recommendation patterns for the generic adapter
  4. Try refreshing the page — RecoMe initializes on page load
The Agent says "Quick mode" but I configured an API key
  1. Open Settings → AI Enhancement
  2. Verify your API key is correct and saved
  3. Check that you selected the right provider
  4. Ensure you have API credits remaining with your provider
Content is being hidden that I want to see
  • Lower the Aggressiveness slider in Settings
  • Check your blocked topics list for overly broad topics
  • Use the 👍 button on content you want to see more of
  • Boost specific topics you enjoy
The extension feels slow
  • RecoMe processes content in the background — initial page loads should not be affected
  • If the popup is slow, try clearing old data (Settings → Clear All Data)
  • Check the DevTools page (popup footer → "DevTools") for performance metrics
  • Disable platforms you don't use to reduce processing
The popup shows "Background service restarting"

Chrome occasionally puts service workers to sleep. RecoMe detects this and shows a degraded-mode banner. The service worker will restart automatically within a few seconds. All your data is preserved.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is RecoMe really free?

Yes, completely free. There's no premium tier, no subscription, and no hidden costs. The optional AI features use your own API key with your own provider, so you control the costs directly.

Does RecoMe send my data anywhere?

No. All data stays in your browser. The only external calls happen if you configure an AI provider, and those go directly from your browser to the AI provider's API. RecoMe has no server.

Will blocking topics affect my actual platform accounts?

No. RecoMe works as a visual overlay. It hides content in your browser but doesn't interact with platform APIs or modify your account settings. Your YouTube subscriptions, Amazon orders, etc. are not affected.

How is RecoMe different from an ad blocker?

Ad blockers remove advertisements. RecoMe filters recommendations — the content the platform suggests based on its algorithms. RecoMe lets you control what recommendations you see, not just block ads.

Can I use RecoMe on Firefox?

RecoMe includes Firefox compatibility settings (Gecko ID configured), but the primary build target is Chromium browsers. Firefox support is experimental.

How do I reset everything and start fresh?

Go to Settings → scroll to the bottom → "Clear All Data". This removes all preferences, memory, achievements, and stats. You'll go through onboarding again. Consider exporting your data first as a backup.
